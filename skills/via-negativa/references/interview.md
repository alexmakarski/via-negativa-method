# Via Negativa Interview Worksheet

Gathers the minimum information needed to run an audit. Used to seed Full audits;
optional for Quick and Standard. Unknown answers are acceptable, note them as
unknown rather than guessing. Gaps in this worksheet are themselves findings
(they feed Section 16, Information Quality).

Ask for these in conversation; do not make the user fill a literal form. Stop
asking once you have enough to run the requested depth.

## System overview
- System name
- System type (business, startup, product, team, software system, process, investment thesis, personal system)
- Goal: what is the system trying to achieve?
- Current process: how does it actually operate today?

## Flows
- Inputs: what enters the system? (customers, leads, data, capital, inventory, staff)
- Outputs: what leaves it? (revenue, customers, products, decisions, reports)
- Users: who operates it?
- Customers: who benefits from the outputs?

## Economics and incentives
- Revenue model: how does money enter?
- Incentives: how are participants rewarded?
- Metrics: what is currently measured?

## Stress points
- Bottlenecks: where does work slow down?
- Failure points: where does it already break?
- Dependencies: what must keep working? (vendors, platforms, employees, software)
- Known problems: what already hurts?
- Recent changes: what changed lately?

## Pressure to add
- Proposed additions: what are people trying to add? (features, tools, automation, vendors, staff)

## Quick risk scan
- Largest risk
- Largest dependency
- Largest assumption
- Largest complexity source
- Largest incentive problem

## Depth recommendation

Based on what surfaces:
- Limited info or a small system -> Quick
- Meaningful context, single owner -> Standard
- High stakes, concentration, key-person dependency, visible tail risk, or
  incentive misalignment -> Full

Guiding question before adding anything: what should be removed first?
